## 3. Accounts Payable (AP)

### Invoice Submission
All vendor invoices must be submitted to ap@moongas.com. German domestic invoices must comply with §14 UStG requirements (must include supplier's VAT number, customer's address, invoice date, unique invoice number, and VAT breakdown).

**Electronic invoicing:** Moongas accepts invoices in ZUGFeRD and XRechnung formats from German suppliers. For cross-border invoices, standard PDF is accepted.

### Approval Thresholds (EUR equivalent)

| Invoice Value | Approval Required |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Up to EUR 2,000 | Department supervisor |
| EUR 2,001–EUR 10,000 | Department head / site manager |
| EUR 10,001–EUR 50,000 | Division VP |
| EUR 50,001–EUR 200,000 | CFO |
| Above EUR 200,000 | CFO + CEO |
| CAPEX (any value) | CFO + CEO + Supervisory Board (above EUR 500K) |

### Payment Terms
Standard net-30 from invoice receipt. Supplier agreements with Skonto (early payment discount) terms: standard 2% net-14 where offered. All Skonto claims processed in the Tuesday payment run.

**Payment runs:** Twice weekly — Monday and Thursday (Stuttgart entity). Other entities process per their local cash management schedule coordinated with Stuttgart treasury.

## 4. Accounts Receivable (AR)

Moongas invoices customers upon shipment confirmation (for component supply) or at programme milestones (for MoonAuto projects and long-term PolyCast supply programmes).

**Standard credit terms:** Net-30 for established customers; net-45 for new customers in the first year (with credit check required, approved by CFO).

**Collections:** Finance team monitors AR aging weekly. Overdue accounts at 45+ days are flagged to the relevant Sales contact. At 60+ days, a formal payment reminder (Mahnung) is issued per German commercial practice. At 90+ days, the CFO reviews and Legal is engaged if required.

**Letter of Credit (LC):** Required for first-time orders from customers outside EU, UK, USA, Japan, and Australia. Confirmed LC required for orders above EUR 250,000 from any non-OECD customer.

## 5. Expense Reimbursement

### Submission
Expense claims must be submitted in SAP Concur within **21 days** of the expense being incurred. All claims require original receipts (digital via Concur app is accepted). Claims without receipts above EUR 10 will not be reimbursed.

### Travel Policy

| Category | Policy |
|----------|--------|
| Air travel < 4 hours | Economy class |
| Air travel 4–8 hours | Economy class; Premium Economy permitted for Director+ with VP approval |
| Air travel > 8 hours | Business Class for Director+; Economy for all others unless medical exception |
| Hotels (Germany) | Up to EUR 180/night (Stuttgart), EUR 150/night (other German cities) |
| Hotels (USA) | Up to USD 250/night |
| Hotels (Japan) | Up to JPY 25,000/night |
| Hotels (other international) | Up to EUR 200/night equivalent |
| Daily subsistence (domestic Germany) | Per Bundesreisekostengesetz Verpflegungspauschale (EUR 28/day) |
| Daily subsistence (international) | Per German Foreign Travel Per Diem tables |

### Non-Reimbursable
- Alcohol (except as part of an approved client entertainment event)
- First-class flights under any circumstances
- Hotel upgrades, in-room entertainment
- Personal travel additions or extensions
- Traffic fines

## 6. Procurement and Purchase Orders

All vendor engagements above EUR 3,000 require a Purchase Order (PO) raised in SAP S/4HANA before any work commences. Emergency exceptions require Finance Controller written approval within 24 hours.

**How to raise a PO:**
1. Submit a Purchase Requisition (PR) in SAP S/4HANA
2. Attach scope of work, vendor quote, and cost centre code
3. Route for approval per threshold table above
4. Procurement team converts approved PR to PO and issues to vendor
5. PO number must appear on all invoices

**Supplier Qualification:** All new suppliers above EUR 5,000 annual spend must complete the Moongas Supplier Self-Assessment Questionnaire (covering quality, HSE, financial stability, and compliance). Suppliers to automotive or aerospace programmes must additionally pass a quality audit.

---

## 7. Capital Expenditure (CAPEX)

CAPEX is defined as expenditure on assets with useful economic life >1 year and value >EUR 1,000 per item.

**CAPEX approval levels:**
- Up to EUR 50,000: Site Manager + CFO
- EUR 50,001–EUR 500,000: CFO + CEO
- Above EUR 500,000: CFO + CEO + Supervisory Board resolution

CAPEX projects are tracked monthly in the CAPEX register maintained by Finance Controller. All CAPEX is subject to post-investment review at 12 and 24 months against original business case.

---

## 8. Inter-Company Transactions

All transactions between Moongas entities (e.g., Stuttgart supplying components to the Detroit plant, or Stuttgart charging management services to subsidiaries) must be priced at arm's-length rates per the Group Transfer Pricing Policy (document available from Finance). Transfer pricing documentation is maintained annually for each entity.
