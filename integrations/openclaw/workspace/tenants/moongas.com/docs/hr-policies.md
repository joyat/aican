## 1. Employment Principles

Moongas Industries AG is an equal opportunity employer committed to fair, respectful, and transparent employment practices in every country where we operate. We comply with all applicable local employment laws and additionally hold ourselves to the standards set out in the UN Global Compact, the VDA Code of Conduct, and the German Supply Chain Due Diligence Act (Lieferkettensorgfaltspflichtengesetz, LkSG).

We do not discriminate on the basis of race, nationality, religion, gender, gender identity, age, disability, marital status, union membership, or any other protected characteristic. Violations of this principle are treated as serious disciplinary matters.

---

## 2. Works Councils (Germany Only)

All three German Moongas sites (Stuttgart HQ, Stuttgart-Feuerbach, and the combined entity) have elected Works Councils (Betriebsräte) as provided under the Works Constitution Act. Works Councils have co-determination rights on:
- Working time arrangements and shift schedules
- Introduction of performance monitoring systems
- Site-level HR policies and disciplinary procedures
- Works agreements (Betriebsvereinbarungen)

The Works Council is an independent body representing employee interests. Management is required to inform and consult the Works Council before implementing changes that affect the workforce. Current Works Council Chair (Stuttgart HQ): Josef Huber.

## 3. Recruitment Policy

**Internal-first principle:** All roles are posted internally in SAP SuccessFactors for 7 calendar days before external posting, unless the role is for a newly created position or requires specialist skills not present internally.

**Referral Programme:** Employees who refer a successful candidate receive EUR 1,000 (Germany) or local currency equivalent, paid 3 months after the referred employee's confirmed start date.

**Non-Discrimination in Recruitment:** Job advertisements must not specify age, gender, or personal characteristics unless these are genuine occupational requirements. All interview panels of 3+ people must include at least one person of a different gender from the hiring manager.

**Background Checks:** All offers of employment are conditional on satisfactory reference checks (minimum 2 professional references). Roles with access to classified customer drawings or controlled substances (certain chemistry labs in Stuttgart) additionally require criminal background checks in accordance with applicable local law.

## 4. Onboarding

New employees complete a structured onboarding programme:

**Day 1:**
- Collection of equipment (laptop, PPE if applicable, site access card)
- IT setup and system credentials
- Safety induction (production employees: full safety induction on Day 1; office employees: by end of Week 1)
- Meeting with line manager and HR Business Partner

**Week 1–2:**
- Site and department orientation
- Introduction to SAP S/4HANA, quality management system, and relevant standard operating procedures
- Health and safety e-learning modules (mandatory before unsupervised work begins)

**30-Day Review:**
Formal check-in with manager and HR to confirm onboarding completeness, address questions, and confirm probation progress.

**Buddy System:** Each new employee is assigned an experienced colleague from the same team as an onboarding buddy for the first 3 months.

## 5. Performance Management

### Review Cycle
Performance reviews are conducted in November–December each year. Results are communicated in January and form the basis for March salary adjustments and annual bonus payments.

### Rating Scale
- **Exceptional** — Performance significantly exceeds all objectives; recognised as a role model
- **Exceeds Expectations** — Consistently delivers above agreed targets
- **Meets Expectations** — Delivers all agreed objectives to the required standard
- **Partially Meets Expectations** — Some objectives not met; improvement required
- **Does Not Meet Expectations** — Significant performance shortfall; formal action required

### Improvement Process
Employees rated "Partially Meets" receive a written development plan with 60-day review. Employees rated "Does Not Meet" are placed on a formal Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) with 90-day timeline and specific, measurable targets. Continued non-improvement after a PIP results in formal disciplinary proceedings per local law.

## 6. Compensation Policy [RESTRICTED — HR and Management Use Only]

*This section is for line managers, HR, and the CFO only and must not be shared with employees outside these roles.*

Moongas positions total cash compensation (base + bonus) at the **55th–65th percentile of the relevant local manufacturing market**, benchmarked annually against Kienbaum and Willis Towers Watson industrial sector surveys.

**Indicative salary bands by grade (Germany, Stuttgart, FY2025):**

## 7. Disciplinary Policy

Moongas operates a progressive discipline process in all jurisdictions (subject to local law requirements):

1. **Verbal warning** — Issued by line manager; documented in SuccessFactors (not placed in formal employment record at this stage in Germany, per Works Council agreement)
2. **Written warning (Abmahnung)** — Formal written notice placed in the employee's personnel file. Two written warnings within 12 months may lead to termination
3. **Final written warning** — Signals that further breaches will result in termination
4. **Termination** — Following fair process. In Germany, requires compliance with Kündigungsschutzgesetz and consultation with the Works Council

**Gross misconduct** (examples: theft, fraud, data breach, serious safety violation, workplace violence) may result in summary dismissal without prior warnings, following a fair investigation.

## 8. Anti-Corruption and Gifts Policy

Moongas complies with the German Criminal Code (StGB), UK Bribery Act 2010, US Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), and applicable local anti-corruption laws.

- Employees must not offer or accept cash payments or gifts that could influence a business decision
- Gifts to or from a single supplier or customer must not exceed EUR 35 per occasion
- Any gift above this threshold must be declared to the line manager and HR
- Hospitality (dinners, events) must be proportionate, transparent, and declared in the gifts register
- Entertainment of government officials requires advance approval from the CFO

## 9. Training and Qualifications

Technical training is mandatory for production employees:
- Machine operator qualification programmes are site-specific and documented in the Quality Management System
- Refresher training required annually for safety-critical processes
- Cross-site skills development: employees may apply for training rotations at other Moongas facilities (with manager and HR approval)

**Mandatory training for all employees (annually):**
- Information security awareness
- Anti-corruption and compliance
- Health and safety refresher
- (Production employees) Site safety update
