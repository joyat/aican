## 1. Who We Are

Moongas Industries AG is a German precision engineering and industrial automation company headquartered in Stuttgart, Baden-Württemberg. We design and manufacture high-tolerance precision components, custom industrial automation systems, and specialty alloy castings for clients in the automotive, aerospace, medical, and energy sectors worldwide.

We are approximately 1,200 employees across six countries, with annual revenue of EUR 487M as of FY2024.

---

## 2. History

### 1987 — Founding
Klaus-Dieter Hoffmann founded Mondgas Zerspanungstechnik GmbH (renamed Moongas Industries AG in 2001) in Stuttgart-Mitte with a five-person team and two CNC machining centres. The business began as a small-batch precision machining workshop serving local Tier 2 automotive suppliers in the Stuttgart region.

### 1990–1997 — Growth as Automotive Tier 2 Supplier
The expansion of the Stuttgart automotive corridor drove rapid growth. By 1995, Moongas had become a qualified supplier to Robert Bosch GmbH and ZF Friedrichshafen AG. A second CNC facility was leased in Stuttgart-Feuerbach in 1996, doubling capacity.

### 1998 — ISO 9001 Certification
Moongas received ISO 9001:1994 certification — a prerequisite for direct supply contracts with Daimler-Benz (now Mercedes-Benz) and Porsche AG. This opened a tier of contract opportunities that defined the company's scale trajectory.

### 2001 — Reorganisation as AG and Rebranding
The GmbH was restructured into Moongas Industries AG to facilitate institutional investment and international expansion. The "Moongas" name was retained as a strategic brand — the founders believed precision manufacturing, like moonlight, required patience, steadiness, and reflection rather than the brute energy of sun.

### 2004 — MoonAuto Division Launch
Recognising that customers needed not just precision components but complete assembly solutions, Klaus-Dieter Hoffmann invested EUR 8M in establishing the **MoonAuto** industrial automation division. The first MoonAuto turnkey assembly line was delivered to a Tier 1 automotive body shop in Bavaria. Today, MoonAuto has delivered over 340 turnkey automation projects globally.

### 2007 — Detroit Joint Venture
Moongas entered a 51/49 joint venture with US automotive parts group **LakeShore Manufacturing Inc.** to establish a CNC machining facility in **Detroit, Michigan**, serving North American automotive OEMs. This JV was fully acquired by Moongas in 2014 after LakeShore's parent company exited manufacturing.

### 2010 — Tokyo Office
Opened a sales and application engineering office in **Tokyo** to serve Japanese electronics, precision instruments, and robotics manufacturers. Key clients in Japan include several Tier 1 electronics manufacturers and a major medical devices group (names protected by NDA).

### 2013 — São Paulo Plant
Established a precision manufacturing facility in **São Paulo, Brazil**, to serve Brazilian heavy industry, energy (pre-salt oil and gas), and mining clients. The São Paulo plant now employs 140 people and sources approximately 15% of group revenue from Latin American customers.

### 2017 — PolyCast Acquisition (Warsaw)
Acquired **PolyCast Sp. z o.o.**, a Warsaw-based aluminium and titanium casting specialist, for EUR 34M. PolyCast brought NADCAP-certified aerospace casting capabilities and an established customer base in Polish and European aerospace manufacturing. The Warsaw site now operates as the **Moongas Specialty Alloys division**.

### 2019 — IATF 16949 and AS9100D Certification
Completed group-wide IATF 16949 (automotive quality management) and AS9100D (aerospace quality management) certifications across the Stuttgart and Warsaw sites — enabling direct supply relationships with aerospace primes.

### 2020 — Singapore Office
Opened a **Singapore** sales and application engineering office to coordinate ASEAN contracts and manage relationships with semiconductor equipment manufacturers and electronics OEMs in the region.

### 2022 — ISO 13485 Certification (Medical Devices)
Stuttgart facility certified to ISO 13485 (medical device quality management), opening a new client vertical in surgical instrument components and implantable device housings.

### 2023 — Industry 4.0 Capital Investment
Completed a EUR 120M capital investment at the Stuttgart HQ facility: full MES (Manufacturing Execution System) deployment, real-time OEE dashboards at every production cell, predictive maintenance sensors on all CNC machines, and digital twin infrastructure for complex component programmes. This placed Moongas's Stuttgart facility among the most digitally integrated precision machining operations in Europe.

### 2024 — EV Battery Enclosure Programme
Secured a five-year supply agreement (EUR 180M total) with a leading European EV manufacturer for aluminium battery enclosure castings from the Warsaw PolyCast facility. This is the company's largest single contract to date and reflects the strategic pivot of the Specialty Alloys division toward the EV supply chain.

### 2025 — Current State
1,200 employees. EUR 487M revenue. Six manufacturing and sales locations across four continents. Three product divisions (Precision CNC, MoonAuto, Specialty Alloys). Board exploring minority investment or strategic partnership to fund North American and ASEAN expansion.
