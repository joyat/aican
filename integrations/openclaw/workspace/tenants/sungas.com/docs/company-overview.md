## 1. Who We Are

Sungas Technologies Ltd. is a Singapore-headquartered enterprise SaaS company that builds cloud, AI, and security platforms for mid-market and enterprise clients across the Asia-Pacific, EMEA, and GCC regions. Our software helps organisations in financial services, healthcare, and government manage data securely, automate complex workflows, and make smarter decisions with AI.

We are approximately 650 employees strong, operating from five countries, with annual recurring revenue of SGD 94M as of FY2025.

---

## 2. History

### 2011 — Founding
Arjun Mehta (CEO) and Li Wei Chen (CTO) co-founded Sungas Technologies in Singapore after spending several years as enterprise software engineers at Salesforce's APAC division. The name "Sungas" reflects the founders' belief that software should be a source of energy and clarity for organisations — "sun" for light, "gas" for the energy that drives things forward.

Seed funding of SGD 2.4M came from SGInnovate and a small consortium of Singapore-based angel investors. The original team of eight engineers worked out of a co-working space in Marina One.

### 2012 — First Enterprise Customer
Signed first paying customer: a Singapore-based regional insurer that needed a compliant cloud data warehouse to replace ageing on-premises infrastructure. This deal validated the core DataVault product concept and generated the first SGD 280K in ARR.

### 2013 — SunCloud Platform v1.0 Launch
Released the full SunCloud Platform with DataVault, a basic WorkflowEngine, and five connector integrations. Secured two additional BFSI clients in Singapore. Headcount grew to 28.

### 2015 — Series A (SGD 6M)
Led by Vertex Ventures SEA. Used to build out the Bangalore R&D hub and accelerate product development. First hires in Bangalore: 12 engineers. Beginning of the India engineering culture that defines Sungas today.

### 2016 — ANZ Banking Group Contract & Australia Entry
Signed a three-year enterprise contract with ANZ Banking Group — the first Fortune 500 client. To support ANZ and the broader Australian market, opened a commercial office in Sydney. This was a defining moment that shifted Sungas's market positioning from regional startup to credible enterprise vendor.

### 2018 — Series B (USD 18M) & London Office
Series B led by Balderton Capital (London). Used to open the London office, hire an EMEA sales team, and accelerate IntegrationHub to 250+ connectors. By end of 2018, Sungas had 180 employees across three countries.

### 2019 — MAS RegTech Recognition
Recognised by the Monetary Authority of Singapore (MAS) as a RegTech solution provider, placing Sungas on the preferred vendor list for Singapore-regulated financial institutions. This drove significant new business in the SGD 1M+ contract tier.

### 2020 — SunAI Analytics Launch
Launched SunAI Analytics at the SunSummit virtual conference. The ForecastAI and RiskRadar modules were built on a 30-month R&D investment from the Bangalore team. Within 12 months, SunAI was contributing 22% of new ARR from existing customers.

### 2021 — COVID-19 Response and Remote-First Infrastructure
Pivoted rapidly to 100% remote operations during the pandemic. Invested in async-first collaboration tools, distributed engineering practices, and mental health support. Retained 96% of workforce through the period and grew headcount by 18%.

### 2022 — Dubai Office & CipherNest Acquisition
Opened the Dubai office to serve growing GCC demand from financial institutions navigating Vision 2030 digital transformation. Acquired CipherNest Ltd. (UK, founded 2016) for GBP 8.2M — a specialist in data-loss prevention (DLP) technology. CipherNest's IP became the foundation of the SunShield Security product line.

### 2023 — SunShield GA Launch & GDPR Compliance Module
Launched SunShield Security at Gartner Security Summit. The GDPR Compliance Module won the "Best Compliance Innovation" award from the APAC CISO Alliance. SunShield grew 68% YoY in its first full fiscal year.

### 2024 — 500-Employee Milestone & Series C Planning
Crossed 500 employees. ARR reached SGD 78M. Board approved Series C process targeting USD 40M+ to fund expansion into Japan, South Korea, and the US enterprise market. Series C expected to close in H1 FY2026.

### 2025 — Current State
650 employees. ARR SGD 94M. 185 enterprise clients across 28 countries. Three mature product lines (SunCloud, SunAI, SunShield). Series C underway.

## 3. How We Are Organised

Sungas operates with a product-led, cross-functional structure:

- **Product & Engineering**: Three product lines (SunCloud, SunAI, SunShield), each with a dedicated Product Manager and Engineering lead. Bangalore is the primary engineering location; Singapore houses product leadership and senior architects.
- **Sales & Customer Success**: Organised by region — APAC (Singapore + Sydney), EMEA (London), GCC (Dubai). Customer Success is centralised in Singapore and embedded in key accounts.
- **Finance**: Centralised in Singapore, with the CFO (Priya Nair) and Finance Controller (Benjamin Lim) overseeing all entities. Financial reporting follows Singapore FRS.
- **HR / People**: Led by Chief People Officer Sophie Hartmann from Singapore. HR Business Partners are embedded in Bangalore and London.
- **Legal & Compliance**: Two-person team in Singapore, supported by external law firms in each jurisdiction.
- **IT & Infrastructure**: Led by Head of IT Aarav Shah. Manages internal systems, device management, and coordinates with the SunShield product team on internal security posture.

## 4. Our Clients

Sungas serves approximately 185 enterprise clients as of April 2025. Key industry verticals:

- **Financial Services (BFSI)**: 61% of ARR — banks, insurers, asset managers, and fintechs across Singapore, Australia, UK, UAE, and India
- **Healthcare**: 18% of ARR — hospital groups and health-tech platforms using SunCloud for compliant data management and SunAI for clinical analytics
- **Government & Public Sector**: 12% of ARR — Singapore statutory boards, Australian state government agencies
- **Other (logistics, manufacturing, retail)**: 9% of ARR

Notable publicly referenceable clients include ANZ Banking Group (since 2016) and a Singapore Statutory Board (MOU signed 2023, details subject to NDA).
