## 1. Welcome

Welcome to Sungas Technologies. This handbook covers the policies, benefits, and expectations that apply to all employees, regardless of location or seniority. It is your first reference point for how we work. Where local employment law differs from what is written here, local law prevails.

For questions not answered here, contact your HR Business Partner or write to people@sungas.com.

---

## 2. Our Culture and Values

We hire for values as much as skill. Every employee is expected to demonstrate:

1. **Build for trust** — Customers trust us with sensitive data. We do not cut corners on security or compliance.
2. **Move with intent** — We work fast, but we think before we ship.
3. **Own the outcome** — If something breaks or underdelivers, we own the fix.
4. **Grow each other** — We mentor, support, and give honest feedback.
5. **Think global, serve local** — We respect cultural differences across our 5 offices and 28 client countries.

## 3. Working Hours and Flexibility

Sungas operates on a **results-based flexible work policy**. There is no mandatory 9-to-5. Each team defines its collaboration hours — the windows during which everyone is expected to be online and responsive. Outside those windows, you manage your own schedule.

**Default collaboration hours by location:**

| Location | Collaboration window (local time) |
|----------|----------------------------------|
| Singapore (HQ) | 10:00 – 17:00 SGT (Mon–Fri) |
| Bangalore | 10:00 – 17:00 IST (Mon–Fri) |
| London | 09:30 – 16:30 GMT/BST (Mon–Fri) |
| Sydney | 09:30 – 16:30 AEST/AEDT (Mon–Fri) |
| Dubai | 09:30 – 16:30 GST (Mon–Sun as locally required) |

**Hybrid Work:** Singapore HQ requires a minimum of 3 days in-office per week. Bangalore R&D hub requires 4 days in-office. London, Sydney, and Dubai offices operate on flexible hybrid arrangements agreed with line managers.

**Remote Work from Other Countries:** Requests to work remotely from outside your home country for more than 14 consecutive calendar days require HR and Legal approval due to tax and employment law implications.

## 4. Leave Policy

### Annual Leave
- **Employees in Singapore, Bangalore, London, Sydney:** 18 days per year (accruing from start date)
- **Dubai:** 22 days per year (in line with UAE Labour Law)
- Leave increases by 2 days per year of service up to a maximum of 25 days
- Up to 5 days of unused leave may be carried over to the next calendar year; the rest is forfeited unless approved by the CPO

### Sick Leave
- 14 days paid sick leave per year (Singapore statutory minimum; matched globally)
- Hospitalisation leave: up to 60 days per year (hospitalisation certificate required)

### Parental Leave
- **Primary caregiver (birth or adoption):** 16 weeks fully paid
- **Secondary caregiver:** 4 weeks fully paid
- Singapore-domiciled employees may claim Government-Paid Parental Leave (GPPL) top-up through MOM

### Public Holidays
Employees observe the public holidays of the country they are primarily based in. Singapore HQ follows MOM-gazetted public holidays. When a public holiday falls on a weekend, a substitute weekday is granted.

### Leave Requests
All leave must be applied for through the HR system (Workday). Requests should be submitted with at least 5 business days' notice for leave of 3 days or more. Emergency leave is handled with line manager discretion.

## 5. Compensation and Benefits

### Salary Reviews
Annual performance reviews are held in October–November, with salary adjustments effective 1 January. Mid-year reviews are available for promotions. All compensation is confidential between the employee, their manager, and HR.

### Employee Stock Options (ESOP)
All permanent employees at Grade 3 and above receive stock option grants at the time of hire and at subsequent annual reviews. Options vest over 4 years (1-year cliff, quarterly thereafter). Current strike price and vesting schedule details are in your individual employment contract. Contact finance@sungas.com for option balance queries.

### Health Insurance
- Singapore: Group health insurance via Great Eastern (inpatient and outpatient, dental top-up available)
- Bangalore: Group mediclaim policy via Star Health; family floater option available
- London: Private medical insurance via Bupa
- Sydney: Group health cover via Medibank (optional top-up available)
- Dubai: Mandatory DHA-compliant health insurance via AXA

### Wellness Allowance
Every employee receives SGD 600 (or local currency equivalent) per year to spend on physical or mental wellness — gym memberships, apps, therapy sessions, fitness equipment. Submit claims via Workday with receipt.

### Learning & Development Budget
SGD 1,500 per employee per year for learning — courses, certifications, conferences, books. Requires manager pre-approval above SGD 300. Submit in Workday > Learning > L&D Budget Request.

## 6. Code of Conduct

All employees are expected to:
- Treat colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and professionalism
- Protect confidential company and customer data — do not share credentials, store data on personal devices, or discuss sensitive information in public spaces
- Disclose actual or potential conflicts of interest to your manager and HR
- Not accept gifts from vendors or clients worth more than SGD 50 in value without manager approval
- Report suspected fraud, data breaches, or ethical violations immediately to legal@sungas.com or anonymously via the Ethics Hotline (ethicshotline.sungas.com)

## 7. Performance and Development

### Performance Reviews
Sungas uses a quarterly check-in model plus an annual review. Check-ins are 30-minute conversations between employee and manager, tracked in Workday. The annual review generates a performance rating (1–5 scale) that feeds into salary and ESOP decisions.

### Career Levels
Engineering: L1 (Junior) → L2 → L3 → L4 (Senior) → L5 (Staff) → L6 (Principal) → L7 (Distinguished)  
Commercial / G&A: Grade 1 (Associate) → Grade 2 → Grade 3 (Senior) → Grade 4 (Manager/Lead) → Grade 5 (Director) → Grade 6 (VP) → Grade 7 (C-Suite)

### Internal Mobility
Sungas actively supports internal transfers. Employees who have completed 12 months in their current role may apply for open roles in other teams or offices. Speak to your HR Business Partner.

## 9. Data Security Responsibilities

Every employee is responsible for data security. Key obligations:
- Use company-managed devices for all work; personal devices may only be used via the approved MDM profile
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA) is mandatory for all systems
- Report lost or stolen devices to IT within 1 hour: helpdesk@sungas.com
- Do not use personal email accounts for work communications
- Do not store client data on personal cloud services (personal Google Drive, Dropbox, etc.)

Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

## 10. Grievances and Disciplinary Process

Sungas is committed to fair and transparent processes.

**Grievances:** Raise concerns first with your line manager. If unresolved within 5 business days, escalate to your HR Business Partner. For sensitive matters (e.g., involving your manager), go directly to people@sungas.com.

**Disciplinary process:** Minor issues are addressed via verbal counselling documented in Workday. Serious issues follow a formal process: written notice → investigation → outcome letter. Gross misconduct may result in immediate termination following an investigation.

Full Grievance and Disciplinary Policy is available in the HR document library on Google Drive.
